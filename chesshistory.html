Some Chess History

Chess is one of the oldest and most popular board games in the world, with a history spanning over a thousand years. It has evolved from early strategic games played in India to the modern version we know today.

6th Century AD - Origins

Chess originated in northern India during the Gupta Empire. It was known as "Chaturanga," which means "four divisions of the military": infantry, cavalry, elephants, and chariotry.

7th-9th Century - Spread to Persia and the Arab World

The game spread to Persia where it was called "Shatranj." After the Islamic conquest, chess spread throughout the Arab world and later into Europe via Spain and Sicily.

19th Century - Organized Competitions

The first official world chess championship was held in 1886, marking the beginning of organized competitive chess.

20th Century - Chess in the Computer Age

The rise of computers revolutionized chess, with programs like Deep Blue defeating world champions, and online chess platforms connecting players worldwide.

Today - Chess for Everyone

Chess remains a global pastime enjoyed both casually and professionally, with millions of players, streaming platforms, and tournaments.
